What are general pregnancy counselling services?

In general pregnancy counselling services, women and men get advice about pregnancy and birth. These services are optional. In Germany, people have a right to get these services. This right is part of the Schwangeren- und Familienhilfeänderungsgesetz (Pregnant Women and Family Assistance Amendment Act). Often, family and social counselling services offer counselling sessions for pregnant women. So do midwives. There are also guidance centres that offer help especially to pregnant women in emergency situations.

You don’t have to be pregnant to get a counselling session. The counselling session can be about many things. Family planning for example. This means if you are planning to get pregnant or if you have questions about contraception. Or if you wish to have children. Then you can get information about starting a family. And of course about pregnancy itself, birth and life after birth with a small child. And about questions on state benefits and services for pregnant women. This means anything that has to do with money. For example, parental support payments or maternity pay. General pregnancy counselling services can also be the first stop for pregnant women before they are referred to a guidance centre that offers pregnancy options counselling.

What is pregnancy options counselling?

In pregnancy options counselling, pregnant women can get help if their pregnancy is unwanted. The staff know a lot and will help her make a decision. To have an abortion or not to have one. They explain to pregnant women what kind of options there are for having an abortion. And who will pay for the abortion. And until which week of pregnancy an abortion is allowed. The staff also explain which benefits women can get if they want to keep the child. For example, which financial benefits they can get. Some guidance centres give information about confidential births. With confidential births, women give birth to a child in secret. Safely in a hospital. Without having to say her name. The child will never know who gave birth to it. In Germany, if a pregnant woman decides to have an abortion, she will need a certificate from a pregnancy options counselling service. Only some guidance centres can give out these certificates.
