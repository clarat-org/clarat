Refugee aid

A lot of people have to flee their country because of war and persecution. And then begin a new life in another country. This is often hard. But in Germany, there is help available.

What is refugee aid?

Refugees who have come to Germany have many questions. About things like the asylum procedure and about rights and laws. How can I find an apartment? When can I work and how can I find a job? Can I study in Germany? Or they have questions about family topics: how can I bring my wife, my husband or my children to Germany? Where can I find support if I or my child are not doing well? Where can I find good childcare or a good school for my child?

In Germany, there are many support services. From the state, from big organisations, from associations and from private people. Many people offer help on a voluntary basis. This means that they support other people and do not earn money for doing this. They just want to help. Because they know that it is hard for refugees when they first come to Germany.

What kind of support services are there?

Some benefits that cost money are available to refugees for free from the state. Things like help from doctors or psychologists. This is medical treatment and therapy. There are also integration courses for refugees. These help refugees to get settled in Germany. A lot of guidance centres offer counselling and advising sessions about many topics free of charge. For example, social counselling. Here, a person takes special care of the worries that a refugee or family has. Or advising sessions about asylum, parenting and career. Or advising sessions about family reunification. Many organisations also help with translation and interpreting.

There are many courses where refugees can do further training. For example, language courses or job preparation courses. Or courses to do during free-time, like sport courses, bicycle lessons or cooking classes. Many volunteers are looking for a refugee who can be their tandem partner. These volunteers helps refugees in day-to-day life. At the same time, they learn about another culture.

In Germany, voluntary work is important. Many people are happy to help refugees. And they are happy when others accept their help.
